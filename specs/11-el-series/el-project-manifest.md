## Project Synopsis

*El* follows a 14-year-old mute Latina girl living in a South Florida apartment complex who starts a dog-walking business after her stepfather loses his job. She befriends her neighbor Sheen, a troubled young woman in an abusive relationship. When Sheen dies under mysterious circumstances officially ruled an overdose, El investigates, navigating a cast of suspicious neighbors, menacing landlords, and her own interior fantasy life. The screenplay blends naturalistic coming-of-age drama with surrealist imagery (vines that bind, fantasy courtroom sequences) and culminates in El finding her literal voice by saying "no" aloud for the first time.

#### EL-035
**Title:** El — Nicholl Fellowship Reader Comments
**Filename:** `EL Nicholl Reader Comments.pdf`
**Date:** 2019-09-05
**Format:** PDF (27 KB)
**Tags:** `FEEDBACK` `COMPETITION`
**Related:** EL-029, EL-031

**Annotation:**
**Academy Nicholl Fellowships in Screenwriting** official reader comments for screenplay #5281 ("El"), Round 1. Two readers provided assessments:

**Reader 1** praised the "vibrant character" of El, "real visual sense," and effective use of symbolic imagery (vines). Identified dog-walking business and Sheen friendship as narrative strengths. Criticized the script as "plot-light" with "no particular stakes," noting that after Sheen's death "the script feels like it loses its way," becoming "increasingly surreal" with unclear fantasy/reality boundaries. **Reader 2** found the "voice of this script imaginative and confident" but "didn't really understand the story" due to unclear reality/fantasy distinctions. Suggested clarifying which elements are El's imagination, exploring the reason for her muteness through fantasy, and reducing repetitive scenes. Questioned whether El actually speaks "no" at the end or merely thinks it.

Both readers independently identified the same core issues: reality/fantasy confusion, structural looseness, and unclear resolution. The feedback represents professional industry assessment of a promising but unresolved work.
